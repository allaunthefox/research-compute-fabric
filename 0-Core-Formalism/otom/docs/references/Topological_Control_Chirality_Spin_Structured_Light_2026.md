# Source Note: Topological control of chirality and spin with structured light

## Bibliographic record

**Journal:** Light: Science & Applications  
**Year:** 2026  
**Volume / article:** 15:214  
**DOI:** `10.1038/s41377-026-02278-6`  
**Source status:** uploaded PDF supplied by project author; live metadata not independently re-queried in this commit environment.

## Abstract-level contribution

The paper demonstrates that higher-order Poincaré modes with tunable Pancharatnam topological charge `ell_p` can deterministically control spin angular momentum and optical chirality in free space while remaining in the paraxial regime.

The central mechanism is a propagation-induced spin-orbit interaction: changing `ell_p` drives a measurable radial separation of circular-polarization components, described as a free-space optical Hall effect. The paper attributes this effect to differential Gouy-phase evolution and radial divergence between the two circular components of an initially spin-balanced vector beam.
