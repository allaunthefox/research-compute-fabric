# Researching The Frequency Matters

This is an experimental repository. It provides experimental results from my research in regards to the associations between matter and frequency. Over time, as my research progresses, I plan to add more content. Eventually leading up to the release of the tools which I use for the computation of these waveforms.

If you desire to attempt experimenting with the .wav files, you must first ensure that your audio output device supports a sampling rate that is sufficient for generating ultrasonic frequencies. The files which I provide are usually offered in two variants: 96.000 Hz sampling rate and 192.000 Hz sampling rate. Higher frequencies appear to be more effective at consistently producing results. However, more research is required here.

Most audio devices support at least 96.000 Hz as a sampling rate. You can change this within the sound device settings of your operating system. 

Experience throughout my research efforts has shown that such ultrasonic frequencies hold the potential to penetrate biological tissue. They induce vibrations akin to those which are produced by the actual compound, thereby affecting biological systems in a way that somewhat resembles the effects of the real compound.

The frequencies produced by these files are restricted to the range between 24.000 Hz and 96.000 Hz for the 192kHz variant. 24.000 Hz and 48.000 Hz for the 96kHz variant. These are practically inaudible, albeit distortions in the speaker membrane movement can lead to crackling in case the volume is set too high.

Whether the frequencies are played through speakers or headphones does not really seem to matter, albeit you need to ensure the playback device does not just cut off inaudible ultrasonic ranges. As far as I am concerned, this is usually not the case, so you are most likely good to go.
